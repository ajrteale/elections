    How it works

    Changes introduced by the Boundary Commission mean that all Peterborough City Council seats were up for election this year. The total number of wards has reduced from 24 to 22 and the number of seats is now 60, rising from 57.

    In many wards residents will have been asked to vote for up to three candidates. The council holds elections three years out of every four and therefore candidates will be elected to serve for four, three or two years using the following system:

    • In those wards where there are three vacant seats, the candidate that gains the most votes will be elected for four years, the candidate receiving the second most votes will be elected for three years and the candidate receiving the third most votes will be elected for two years.
    • In those wards where there are two seats up for election, the candidate receiving the most votes will be elected for four years and the candidate who receives the second highest number of votes will be elected for three years.
    • In those wards where there is one seat up for election, the candidate receiving the most votes will be elected for four years.

    Next year is a fallow year and therefore there will be no local elections.
