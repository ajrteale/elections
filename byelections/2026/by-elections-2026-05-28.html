"All the right votes, but not necessarily in the right order"

Just one by-election on 28th May 2026:

Fairwood

Swansea council, Glamorgan; caused by the death of Conservative councillor Paxton Hood-Williams.

Andrew's Previews is going to be doing a lot of work on Wales over the next month. That is a direct consequence of the Senedd election three weeks ago: nineteen new MSs (ten Plaid Cymru, eight Reform UK and one Labour) were local councillors at the time of that election and have been obliged to give up their previous council seats. So today's poll is a bit of a warmup for that.

The Swansea council area takes in a large area outside the city's built-up area itself, including the noted beauty spot of the Gower peninsula to the west of the city which was the first area in the UK to be designated as an Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty. The Gower National Landscape (as AONBs are now called) includes the open land of Fairwood Common and the Fairwood Park golf club, which has twice hosted the Welsh PGA championship. Together, these give their name to the Fairwood electoral ward - although large parts of the Common are actually in the neighbouring Pennard ward.

The main centres of population here are the villages of Upper Killay, which lies on the main road going west from Swansea into the Gower, and Three Crosses on a hill to the north-west of Upper Killay. Both of these have a population of around 1,200. Three Crosses originally served the local mining industry, but this is long gone and the two villages have developed into Swansea commuter areas.

And Fairwood ward votes like a commuter area. It has had Conservative representation continuously since Swansea council was last reorganised in 1995, and the late Conservative councillor Paxton Hood-Williams had represented the area for many years before his death in March at the age of 82. Hood-Williams was first elected to Swansea council in 2004 after retiring from a senior management job at the Margam steelworks, and he had been in local politics in Fairwood ward for four decades after joining Three Crosses community council in the mid-1980s. His council career culminated in 2024-25 when he was Lord Mayor of Swansea.

Paxton Hood-Williams won his fifth and final term of office in 2022, polling 41% of the vote in Fairwood ward against 32% for Labour and 18% for Plaid Cymru. As a Conservative he was in the minority both on Swansea council, which is run by Labour, and in the wider Gower constituency: the Labour MP here since 2017 has been Tonia Antoniazzi, who chairs the Commons Northern Ireland Affairs committee. The Tories were shut out here in the Senedd election three weeks ago, when the Gŵyr Abertawe constituency returned three MSs from Plaid Cymru, two from Reform UK and one from Labour.

Not the most promising of starts for the defending Conservative candidate David Helliwell, who is seeking to get back onto Swansea council: he previously represented Sketty ward from 2017 until he lost his seat to the Lib Dems in 2022. The Labour candidate is Nigel Evans, who appears to be standing for election for the first time. Plaid Cymru are not standing in this by-election, but their candidate from last time Matthew Ward is back on the ballot nonetheless and this time he is an independent candidate. The Lib Dems' Beth Rowe, independent candidate Paul Dennis (who is vice-chair of Three Crosses community council, and lists previous membership of both the Conservatives and Reform UK over the last twelve months) and Reform UK's Stephen Taylor complete the candidate list here.
