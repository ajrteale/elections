Stort Valley

Uttlesford council, Essex; caused by the resignation of Liberal Democrat councillor Janice Loughlin. There is a village in Essex called Ugley. It has a Women's Institute. Unfortunately the Ugley WI got tired of the jokes and they eventually changed their name to the "Women's Institute Ugley Branch". The annual Ugley Farmers Market, however, is still going strong; as is the Ugley Village Hall, which is one of the four polling stations for today's Uttlesford council by-election in Stort Valley ward. According to the notice of poll, there are 395 Ugley electors. The Stort is a minor river which rises in eastern Hertfordshire and flows south through north-western Essex to meet the Lea at Hoddesdon. It is named after the town of Bishop's Stortford, not the other way round as 16th-century cartographers assumed. The Stort Valley ward covers four parishes to the north of Bishop's Startford, the largest of which is Manuden; three of these parishes are actually in the valley, while Ugley lies to the east on the old road between London and Cambridge. Stort Valley ward is covered by the North West Essex constituency represented by the Conservative Party leader, Kemi Badenoch. The last election here was only last month as part of the Stansted division of Essex county council, which was retained by the Conservatives on new boundaries. This area is part of the Uttlesford district of Essex, which is named after an ancient hundred that once covered the north-west corner of the county. Uttlesford council has been run since 2019 by a residents' party, the Residents for Uttlesford, who have recently fallen into a minority on the council as a result of defection losses. However, in Stort Valley ward the Residents have given a free run to veteran Liberal Democrat councillor Janice Loughlin, who was first elected here in an April 2000 by-election and had continuous service from then until she resigned last month on health grounds; she is being treated for cancer. Loughlin was last re-elected in 2023 with a 58-28 lead over the Conservatives. By a curious coincidence, in both the 2019 and 2023 elections in Stort Valley the same total number of votes was cast (635); not only that, but the Conservative candidates polled exactly the same number of votes on each occasion, 176. Presumably this wasn't the same 176 people. Defending that big Lib Dem lead from 2023 in this by-election is Paul Bawlett, who teaches children with special needs and gives an address in Farnham within this ward. The Conservative candidate is George Braeckman, a young farmer who is a parish councillor in the neighbouring parish of Stansted Mountfitchet. Reform UK's Philip Hoy, who was the runner-up in the county council election here last month, completes the ballot. Camp Hill

Nuneaton and Bedworth council, Warwickshire; caused by the resignation of Labour councillor Sarnjit Kaur Dhillon. Moving into the West Midlands, we come to a town where Reform UK have run rampant over the previous two years. Nuneaton has often returned volatile election results in this century, something which is exacerbated by the fact that many of its wards are demographically similar to each other: when one party wins, it often wins big. In the 2025 Warwickshire county elections and the 2026 Nuneaton and Bedowrth council elections, the big winner was Reform UK who won 15 of the 19 seats up for election in May. The other half of Nuneaton and Bedworth council was elected in 2024 which was a very close result between Labour and the Conservatives, so Reform UK are now the largest party on the council with 15 seats; they have formed a minority administration against 10 Labour councillors plus this vacancy, 9 Conservatives and 3 Greens. The new Reform UK council leader, 19-year-old George Finch, is also doing double duty as the leader of Warwickshire county council. Had the former Labour council leader Chris Watkins got his way, this year's election might not have happened at all: Watkins had made a formal request to the government that the 2026 Nuneaton and Bedworth council election be postponed against the wishes of the majority of councillors, and he got no-confidenced by the council for it. Camp Hill is a north-western suburb of Nuneaton, which was developed by the National Coal Board in the 1950s on the grounds of a former 18th-century mansion. The estate has suffered from serious social problems as the coal industry in Nuneaton in died, and Camp Hill was the subject of a major renegeration scheme in the 2000s. The Camp Hill ward which existed at the time of the 2021 census had a very working-class demographic profile; boundary changes for the 2024 election removed the eastern part of the ward, including Judkins Quarry whose spoil heap - the so-called "Mount Judd" is a landmark for locals and for travellers on the West Coast main line. In Nuneaton and Bedworth council elections Camp Hill was generally in the Labour column up to 2024, but it did return a BNP councillor in 2008 - the BNP councillor resigned the following year due to work commitments and Labour gained the resulting by-election - and the ward voted Conservative in 2021. The outgoing ward councillor Sarnjit Kaur Dhillon, also known as Sharon Dhillon, was first elected in 2024; she stood down on health grounds just after the 2026 local elections. Reform UK won Camp Hill very easily in May with exactly twice the Labour vote, 740 votes against 370: the percentage shares were 46% for Reform UK, 23% for Labour and 18% for the Greens. Reform UK also won the larger Camp Hill division of Warwickshire county council in May, gaining that seat from the Conservatives. The Nuneaton parliamentary seat last went to the polls in 2024 before all this blew up, and that constituency has voted for the government at every election since 1983 with the exception of 1992. Its first-term Labour MP is former North Warwickshire councillor Jodie Gosling. It's in that context that Labour will attempt to defend Camp Hill ward in this by-election. They have reselected Eric Amaechi, the councillor who lost his seat here in May; he works for the NHS in cybersecurity, and he had been first elected to the council in 2024 after a number of previous failed attempts in other wards. Reform UK have gone for Peter Gilbert, who previously served from 2017 to 2025 as a Conservative member of Warwickshire county council representing Bedworth West division; Gilbert has only been in the party for about five minutes, as he was a Conservative candidate for Exhall ward in last month's Nuneaton and Bedworth election. There is no Green challenge this time and only one other candidate has come forward: that's Hari Khela for the Conservatives. Farnworth

Halton council, Cheshire; caused by the resignation of Labour councillor Aimee Skinner. Our final three English polls this week are all in territory which was once Lancashire but no longer. Much of southern Lancashire became part of either Greater Manchester or Merseyside in the 1974 reorganisation, but the town of Widnes ended up in Cheshire as part of the Borough of Halton, which combined it with Runcorn on the far side of the Mersey. It's in the Widnes half of the borough that we find Farnworth ward, which covers the north-east corner of Widnes town. The southern boundary of this ward is the arrow-straight railway line between Liverpool and Manchester via Warrington, and just outside the ward boundary is Widnes railway station - which originally opened as Farnworth (Widnes) railway station. Here we find a plaque commemorating that Paul Simon's song Homeward Bound was written at the station in 1965 - although it's fair to say there's some controversy over this, and after I told this story in a Preview some years ago I got a comment from someone who claimed to have made the whole Homeward Bound/railway station thing up. It's a great song, though. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=R4EnErMCKrI In the 2021 census Farnworth made the top 60 wards in England and Wales for Christianity (68.4%), which is a regular feature of the census return for north-west England. The ward has high levels of owner-occupation, and easy access not just to the railway line into Liverpool but also to the M62 motorway. Since the 2024 general election Widnes has been in a constituency entirely north of the River Mersey, with the name of Widnes and Halewood. This and the predecessor Halton constituency have been represented since 1997 by long-serving Labour backbencher Derek Twigg, who had no trouble being re-elected in 2024 for his eighth term of office. Halton council was one of those districts where Reform UK ran rampant in May, but where Labour had done so well in the previous two years that their council majority was secure. Of the 19 Halton seats up for election this year, Reform UK won 16, the Lib Dems held one and just two seats stayed with Labour: that gave an overall council of 32 Labour, 16 Reform UK, 3 Lib Dems and 3 independents. One of the seats which Labour held onto was Farnworth, which was an open seat going into this year's election after the outgoing Labour councillor Val Hill died shortly before the end of her term. But the final Labour majority here against the turquiose tide was just one vote, with new Labour councillor Luke Williams defeating the Reform UK candidate by 837 votes to 836. In percentage terms Labour and Reform UK both scored 36%, with the Greens third on 14%. The previous three results here had been straight fights betwen the Conservatives and Labour, with very one-sided results in Labour's favour. We now have a second vacancy in Farnworth ward this year, after Aimee Skinner handed in her notice just after the May election for personal reasons. Skinner had represented this ward since the all-out election in 2021, when she was still known by her previous surname of Teeling, and she was re-elected for a second term in 2023. Accordingly, the winner of this by-election will need to seek re-election next year. Trying to defend this seat for Labour will be Louise Nolan, who is trying to get back onto Halton council after losing her seat in the neighbouring Halton View ward in May. Nolan had previously represented that ward since 2018, originally being elected under her previous surname of Whitley. Reform UK have changed their candidate to Christopher Charlton-Kingsley, who is a travel agent. The Green Party candidate is Kiel Oldfield, who stood in May in Highfield ward. Also on the ballot are Phil Harper for the Conservatives and Dan Clarke, fresh from his deposit-losing performance in last week's Makerfield by-election, for the Libertarian Party.
