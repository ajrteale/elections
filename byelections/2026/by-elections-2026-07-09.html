Two by-elections on Tuesday 7th July 2026:

Haslemere

Surrey county council; and

Haslemere West

Waverley council, Surrey; both caused by the death of Liberal Democrat councillor John Robini. For a rare Tuesday by-election this week we are on the southern edge of the county of Surrey, close to the point where Surrey, Hampshire and Sussex all meet. The town of Haslemere is up in the hills, on the direct railway line between London and Portsmouth. The A3 road passes to the west of Haslemere and travels under those hills in the Hindhead Tunnel, whose southern portal is part of this ward. Haslemere's population started to grow in the 19th century with the coming of the railway, and it attracted wealthy commuters with a taste for the arts. Many buildings in the town are in the Arts and Crafts style. It says something that the writers of Yes Minister and Yes Prime Minister thought that Sir Humphrey Appleby lived in Haslemere. Presumably he would have caught the commuter train to Waterloo for another day in the offices of the Department of Adminstrative Affairs (and, in time, 10 Downing Street) running rings around Jim Hacker. As a patron of the arts, Sir Humphrey might well have enjoyed the Haslemere early music festival which was organised for many years by the Dolmetsch family. Arnold Dolmetsch, who lived in Haslemere from 1917, was a French musician who had come to London in the 1880s to study at the Royal College of Music and he became an enthusiast for early music and vintage instruments. This included the revival of the recorder, both as a serious musical instrument and as a gateway drug for children to learn about reading and performing music. If you tried to play the recorder in school, you have the Dolmetsches to thank for that: Arnold Dolmetsch and his son Carl ran a firm making recorders here in Haslemere for most of the 20th century. Haslemere was given a borough charter by Elizabeth I, which made it one of the rotten boroughs which were entitled to elect two MPs up until the First Reform Act of 1832. In more years the town has had an unbroken line of Conservative MPs for over a century, with the current incumbent being Greg Stafford who won the first election for the Farnham and Bordon constituency in 2024. Stafford's father's family were originally from Haslemere, where his grandparents ran a sweetshop. Haslemere's local government is being reorganised, and today's two by-elections here are to the old local government structure which will be swept away next April. They follow the death in May of John Robini, a former police officer who was a long-serving Liberal Democrat activist in this corner of Surrey. Robini was first elected in 2003 as a Lib Dem member of Waverley council, which is based in Godalming and covers a large chunk of Surrey's southern rural fringe; but he lost his seat in Haslemere Critchmere and Shottermill ward to the Conservatives in 2007 and didn't get it back until 2019. In 2023 this ward was redrawn with the new name of Haslemere West, and it returned the Lib Dem slate by the emphatic margin of 64-22 over the Conservatives. John Robini had also been elected to Surrey county council in 2021 after many years of trying, defeating the Conservatives by 50-43 in the Haslemere county division. This division helpfully has the same boundaries as the Haslemere ward of the new West Surrey council, which went to the polls in May; this returned the Lib Dem slate with 45% of the vote, against 22% for the Conservatives and 16% for Reform UK, and its Lib Dem councillors Oli Leach and Terry Weldon will take up their full roles next April once the reorganisation is complete. The two by-elections to replace Robini have a large crossover of candidates, with only the defending Lib Dems selecting different candidates for both vacancies - they have gone for the two shadow West Surrey councillors for Haslemere. For the county by-election in Haslemere division the Lib Dem candidate is Terry Weldon, who previously campaigned against apartheid in his native South Africa and now works part-time delivering medicines for a local pharmacy. Weldon is already a Waverley councillor for Haslemere East ward, so the district by-election in Haslemere West needs a different Lib Dem candidate: he is Oli Leach, who topped the poll here in May by a large margin and runs a coffee shop and wine bar in the town. The Conservative candidate in both by-elections is Tony Byfield, a politicial statistician who was an octochamp and quarter-finalist in series 89 of Countdown (covering the first half of 2024); Byfield has spent time in different countries as an elections observer, and in May's West Surrey council election he was the runner-up in Haslemere ward. Also returning from May's election in Haslemere is Reform UK's Barb Witt, an American by birth who is an evangelical Christian preacher. Completing both ballots is the Green Party's Thomas Shrive.

Suffield Park

North Norfolk council; caused by the resignation of Liberal Democrat councillor Emma Tooke. Our East Anglian by-election this week is in the self-styed Gem of the Norfolk Coast, the seaside resort of Cromer. Suffield Park ward is the eastern part of Cromer, covering the East Cliff, the lighthouse, the town's secondary school Cromer Academy and the local hospital. The present lighthouse dates from 1833 and was originaly half a mile from the cliff-edge; as a result of coastal erosion, it's a lot closer now. The ward name is a reference to the Lords Suffield, who owned a significant amount of land in Cromer back in the day including the stately home of Cromer Hall, which is not in this ward. We should note in particular here Edward Harbord, the third Lord Suffield, who was prominent in the campaign to abolish slavery and piloted the Slavery Abolition Act 1833 through the Lords, and his son Charles Harbord, the fifth Lord, who was a close friend of Edward VII and briefly held government office under Gladstone in 1886 as Master of the Buckhounds. Now there's a government job you don't get any more. Parliamentary constituency: North Norfolk Norfolk county council division: Cromer

Kinwarton

Stratford-on-Avon council, Warwickshire; caused by the resignation of Liberal Democrat councillor Thomas Holmes. Our next poll is in the Warwickshire countryside, to the north-west of Stratford-on-Avon and to the north-east of Alcester. Indeed, Kinwarton parish is mostly a north-eastern part of Alcester which has spilled over the town boundary: the town's secondary school, Alcester Academy, is part of this ward. The Kinwarton ward extends along the valley of the River Alne to the village of Aston Cantlow, and then moves south-east to take in the railway junction at Wilmcote. These areas are associated with Mary Arden, the mother of William Shakespeare, who was born in Wilmcote in 1536. Her sixteenth-century farmhouse still exists and is now in the hands of the Shakespeare Birthplace Trust, which still runs the place as a working farm. Unfortunately, it is no longer open to the public. This area is part of the Lib Dem-controlled Stratford-on-Avon district and is also covered by the Stratford-on-Avon constituency, which was gained from the Conservatives in 2024 by Lib Dem MP Manuela Perteghella; she defeated Tory candidate Chris Clarkson, who was attempting to do the chicken run from his previous seat of Heywood and Middleton. The most recent poll here was the 2025 Warwickshire county council election; most of Kinwarton ward is in the Lib Dem-held Alcester county division, but Aston Cantlow is included in the Studley county division which voted Reform UK last year in a close three-way finish. Kinwarton ward was gained by the Lib Dems in 2023, when Thomas Holmes won by 57-33 over the Conservatives; the Tories had won the ward in previous years, although boundary changes confuse the issue. Holmes had stood for the county council the previous year, contesting a by-election in Arden division without success. After working for some time in Manuela Perteghella's constituency office, Holmes tendered his resignation from Stratford-on-Avon council last month. He has moved to Canberra in Australia to work for David Pocock, whom readers might remember as a fixture of the Australian rugby union team who made 83 appearances for his country between 2008 and 2019; after retiring from the sport Pocock entered politics and he has served since 2022 as an independent member of the Australian Senate, representing the Australian Capital Territory. Defending the resulting by-election for the Lib Dems is Tina Ballinger, who is a nursery nurse and a Kinwarton parish councillor; she is hoping to join on Stratford-on-Avon council her son Tom, who won a by-election in Alcester West ward last year. The Conservatives have selected Janine Lee, who is chair of Wilmcote parish council. Also standing here are Andrew Foster for Labour, Lizzee Leedham for the Greens and Reform UK candidate Rachael Chadwick-Harrison, who runs her own accountancy firm.
