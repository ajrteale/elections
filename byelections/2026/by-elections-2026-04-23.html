"All the right votes, but not necessarily in the right order"

One by-election on 23rd April 2026:

Newquay Porth and Tretherras

Cornwall council; caused by the death of Reform UK councillor Kevin Towill.

Spring is in the air and the weather forecast for this week looks good, so let's go to the beach. And we've got a couple of beaches to choose from in today's single Preview, for a ward covering the eastern end of Newquay. The sheltered Porth Beach lies at the end of a narrow inlet between two headlands; Lusty Glaze Beach, which unusually is privately owned down to the low water mark, lies in a natural amphitheatre with the only access being a flight of 133 steps.

The unusual name of Lusty Glaze comes from the Cornish Plustri Glas, referring to green grazing fields. But this could have been a rather industrial area, as Lusty Glaze was intended to be the southern terminus of the abortive St Columb Canal. This canal was the brainchild of John Edyvean, a Cornish engineer: he had intended it to connect a number of parishes to the east and north-east of Newquay to the sea at Lusty Glaze and Mawgan Porth, where the canal cargoes would be dragged up and down inclined planes to reach the sea. The St Columb Canal was authorised by a 1773 Act of Parliament and construction work began, but it was never finished and the whole operation had been abandoned by the time Edyvean died in the 1780s. The remains of the Lusty Glaze inclined plane can still be seen in the cliff-face.

Newquay's economy is based on visitors, but its elections are decided by the people who live here year-round. Porth and Tretherras is the eastern of the three Cornwall wards which are wholly within Newquay parish: Porth lies on its beach, while the Tretherras name is a reference to the Tretherras secondary school whose former pupils include Phillip Schofield. Also within the ward boundary is the small Newquay University Centre, which is part of Cornwall College and specialises in environmentally-focused subjects such as marine science and zoology. It helps that the university centre is next door to Newquay Zoo.

In recent years the population of this area has been growing thanks to the development of Nansledan, a new Newquay suburb which will have something like 4,000 homes once it's finished. Construction work began in 2013 with the Duchy of Cornwall as the developer, and the whole project is very much in line with Charles III's environmental principles with an emphasis on good design and use of local building materials. Charles has taken a keen interest in Nansledan's development: he opened the primary school, Skol Nansledan, in 2020 while he was still Duke of Cornwall, and in February last year the King invited Sir Keir Starmer and his then-deputy Angela Rayner down here for a guided tour. Skol Nansledan is one of the two polling stations for this by-election, and the notice of poll gives its polling district an electorate of 1,544 out of a total for Porth and Tretherras ward of 5,706. This means that the ward's enrolment has grown by 34% since 2017. The Local Government Boundary Commission deliberately drew Porth and Tretherras ward with a low electorate to allow room to grow, but it won't take much more housebuilding here to trigger a boundary review in Cornwall.

Newquay is part of the St Austell and Newquay parliamentary constituency, which has voted for all three of the main Westminster parties since its creation in 2010: it went Lib Dem in 2010, Conservative from 2015 to 2019, and Labour in 2024. The current local MP is Noah Law, a Labour backbencher who came to the Commons from a career as a financial analyst.

The present Newquay Porth and Tretherras ward was created in 2021 as an extension of the former Newquay Tretherras ward, which gained the Porth area from the abolished ward of Newquay Treviglas. Treviglas ward appeared in Andrew's Previews in 2016 and 2018 and was politically volatile, returning UKIP, Liberal Democrat and Conservative councillors in the space of five years: but the old Tretherras ward was consistently in the Conservative column, and Porth and Tretherras ward started off in the same vein. Conservative councillor Kevin Towill, who had been mayor of Newquay in 2012-13 and had represented the old Tretherras ward since 2017, won Porth and Tretherras in 2021 with a 58-25 lead over the Liberal Democrats. Unusually for 2021, Newquay Porth and Tretherras had a Reform UK candidate, who finished in fourth and last place with 3.6%: that was Angie Rayner. No, not that one.

By this point, Kevin Towill was starting to suffer from poor health, with frequent bouts of sickness and stomach issues. The doctors initially thought there was a problem with his mental health and prescribed antidepressants, which did not resolve the symptoms. Eventually Towill refused to leave the Royal Cornwall Hospital until he was properly diagnosed, and an MRI scan in January 2024 revealed a brain tumour. Towill underwent a 12-hour brain operation at Derriford Hospital in Plymouth, which left him unable to walk or talk for six months: his eyesight never fully recovered from this ordeal. In July 2024 Cornwall council voted to grant Towill leave of absence until the May 2025 election, but he continued to be active from his hospital bed and later cited his council laptop as a "lifeline", saying in late 2025 "Being able to keep working helped distract me from everything I was going through."

While Towill was convalescing from this operation, he was challenged for the Conservative nomination in his ward by cabinet member Olly Monk, who was seeking to move here from Newquay Trenance ward. This was one factor in Kevin Towill's defection to Reform UK in February 2025, which made him the first Cornwall councillor from that party. He stood for re-election in May 2025 under his new turquoise colours, and won his third and final term of office with 38% of the vote against 26% for the Lib Dems and 21% for the Conservatives' Monk, who failed in his attempted chicken-run. (Monk's previous ward was lost to the Green Party.)

Kevin Towill passed away in February at the age of 46. He was the trailblazer for what is now a significant Reform UK group on Cornwall council: the 2025 elections returned 28 Reform UK councillors, 26 Lib Dems, 16 independents, 7 Conservatives, 4 Labour and 3 councillors each for Mebyon Kernow and the Green Party. Reform UK have since successfully defended a by-election in the neighbouring Colan and Mawgan ward last December, but they are no longer the largest party after a split enabled the Lib Dems to take over first place. The Liberal Democrats also run Cornwall council in coalition with the main independent group.

Defending Newquay Porth and Tretherras for Reform UK is Lyndon Harrison, a former Newquay town councillor who has previously chaired the town's Chamber of Commerce and Tourism for two years. The Liberal Democrats have reselected their 2025 runner-up Sandy Carter, who is retired from a career in the furniture trade: Carter was mayor of Newquay in 2013-14 and already represents this ward on the town council. The Conservatives' Oli Kimber is a town councillor in St Austell and stood for Cornwall council last year in a St Austell ward: he works in his family's window-cleaning business to earn an honest bob. Also on the ballot are Suzanne Featherstone for Labour, independent candidate Topher Chard who runs two local cafés, and the Green Party's Abigail Horrocks.
