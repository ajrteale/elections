"All the right votes, but not necessarily in the right order"

One by-election on 9th April 2026:

Cliftonville

Kent county council; caused by the disqualification of Reform UK councillor Daniel Taylor.

Your columnist has already started work on previewing the main electoral event of the year, the English local elections, Senedd Cymru and Scottish Parliament elections which are due on 7th May. Candidate nominations in England and Wales close today at 4pm. We do have by-elections to bring you in each of the four weeks before then, but the number is minimal. In fact there is only one poll today, which is at the end of the country.

The Isle of Thanet used to be a genuine island, before the channel separating it from the English mainland silted up and was drained for farmland. It consists of chalk uplands which terminate in sea-cliffs on the northern and eastern coasts. These cliffs are hazardous to North Sea shipping, and at North Foreland - the easternmost point of Kent - a lighthouse has stood since the 17th century. The North Foreland lighthouse was the last Trinity House light to be manned, finally being automated when its keepers retired in 1998; it is now controlled remotely from Harwich.

North Foreland lighthouse lies in the parish of Broadstairs but in the county electoral division of Cliftonville. This mainly consists of eastern suburbs of Margate, hugging the cliffs on the north coast of Thanet. Much of this was developed in the 1930s with the construction of the Palm Bay estate of high-quality housing for the middle classes. Closer to Margate tourism takes over and the clifftop is lined with hotels, many of which were once in the hands of Butlins.

In Thanet council elections that area forms part of Cliftonville West ward, which this division covers about half of. In the 2021 census Cliftonville West made the top 60 wards in England and Wales for residents born in the 2004 EU expansion states of eastern Europe (11.6%), the top 60 wards for residents working in the arts, entertainment and recreation sector (6.1%) and the top 40 wards for households which are privately rented (56.5%). It also had one of the highest unemployment rates in the South East region (6.3%). This ward is not typical of the Cliftonville division as a whole, because the other three Thanet council wards which make up the division have a generally older and more indigenous population.

One of this division's 3½ constituent wards has appeared in this column before, twice. Cliftonville East ward first came to the attention of this column in May 2013, as the result of a scandal which ended the political career of Sandy Ezekiel who had been the Conservative leader of Thanet council. The 2011 Thanet election had returned a hung council, and Ezekiel initially formed a minority Conservative administration: but this was deposed in 2012 when independent councillors, who held the balance of power, withdrew their support and installed a Labour minority administration. It then came out that Sandy Ezekiel had corruptly used the council's inside information to buy two properties in Margate via an intermediary. On 1st March 2013 a jury at Maidstone Crown Court found Ezekiel guilty of four charges of misconduct in public office, and Mr Justice Nicol sentenced him to eighteen months' imprisonment. A £2,000 confiscation order was added later.

Ezekiel did not resign from Thanet council following his conviction and sentence: instead he was disqualified as a councillor three weeks later, when the deadline to appeal against the conviction expired. Because of the timing of the disqualification, the resulting by-election had to be held a week after the 2013 Kent county council elections meaning that the voters of Cliftonville East were dragged out for elections on two consecutive weeks. On 2nd May 2013 UKIP won one of the two county council seats in Margate and Cliftonville division; on 9th May their candidate Rozanne Duncan won the Cliftonville East by-election. UKIP won a second seat here in 2015, the year they took overall control of Thanet council, but come 2019 no UKIP candidates were anywhere to be seen and Cliftonville East has voted Conservative since then. It's clearly the most right-wing part of the Cliftonville county division: of the other wards making up this division, Beacon Road and Cliftonville West voted Labour in 2023 while the small Kingsgate ward returned an independent councillor.

Thanet council has been on a bit of a political rollercoaster ride this century, but it currently has a Labour majority. Labour also hold the parliamentary seat of East Thanet, which is based on the towns of Ramsgate, Broadstairs and Margate. The local MP since 2024 has been Polly Billington, a former BBC political reporter, Labour special adviser and climate campaigner who resigned her previous seat on Hackney council just before the general election was called. She defeated Conservative candidate and North Northamptonshire councillor Helen Harrison, who had lost the parliamentary by-election in Wellingborough a few months previously after her partner Peter Bone was ejected from the Commons (Andrew's Previews 2024, page 49). The outgoing Conservative MP Craig Mackinlay, who had represented the predecessor seat of South Thanet since he defeated one Nigel Farage in 2015, had retired at the 2024 election after developing a serious case of sepsis which had resulted in the amputation of all four of his limbs.

The most recent Cliftonville East by-election in July 2021 (Andrew's Previews 2021, page 297) was held easily by Conservative candidate Charlie Leys, following the resignation of Lesley Game from Thanet council in order to concentrate on her role as a Kent county councillor. Game served as the councillor for Cliftonville division from 2017, when the current division boundaries were introduced, until she became one of the many Kent Conservatives to lose their seats to Reform UK in May 2025. Shares of the vote here last year were 40% for Reform UK candidate Daniel Taylor, 22% for Labour and 20% for the defending Conservatives.

Like Sandy Ezekiel before him, Daniel Taylor's political career has ended in disgrace with a record in the Councillors Behaving Badly file. Taylor was arrested by Kent police in June 2025 and subsequently charged with three offences including making threats to kill his wife. He eventually pleaded guilty to a lesser charge of behaving in a controlling or coercive way towards his wife, with the court being told of a pattern of behaviour going back to 2014. At his sentencing hearing before Margate magistrates in February Taylor, who was already behind bars for breaching his bail conditions, was sentenced to 12 months in prison with the presiding magistrate describing him as man who "can be nasty and violent". This prison sentence disqualifies him from being a councillor for five years.

Trying to pick up the pieces of this for Reform UK is Thanet councillor Marc Rattigan, who is a recent recruit to their banner: Rattigan had previously been elected as a Conservative in a February 2020 by-election for Cliffsend and Pegswell ward (Andrew's Previews 2020, page 39) before he transferred to Cliftonville East ward in 2023. The Labour candidate is Joanne Bright, who has served since 2023 as a Thanet councillor for Beacon Road ward. On that occasion she defeated the Conservative candidate Charlie Leys, who had won the 2021 Cliftonville East ward by-election but then tried to transfer to his home ward: Leys now has the Conservative nomination for this by-election. Yet another Thanet councillor on the ballot is local resident Rob Yates, who was elected in 2019 and 2023 as a Labour councillor for Margate Central ward but is now in the Green Party; he completes the ballot along with the Lib Dems' Mo Shafaei and independent candidate Lucy Gray, who runs a business in Cliftonville but will not be helped by having "(address in Canterbury)" next to her name on the ballot paper. Don't wait up all night for the result, because Thanet will start counting these votes in the morning.
