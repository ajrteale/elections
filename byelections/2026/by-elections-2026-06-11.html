Cippenham Green

Slough council, Berkshire; caused by the resignation of Conservative councillor Robert Stedmond. It's been a long time since Slough featured in this column - not because of the friendly bombs, but because there haven't been any by-elections here for years. The last by-election to Slough council was all the way back in May 2017; you have to go back to March 2012 for a poll that didn't coincide with ordinary local elections elsewhere. We have a lot of catching up to do. The story of Slough council over the last nine years has not been a happy one. To cut a long story short, the council declared bankruptcy in July 2021 and the Commissioners were sent in. They remain in place. Central government then pushed the factory reset button on Slough council, ordering a whole-council election in 2023 on a new electoral map. The 2023 Slough election then delivered huge gains for the Conservatives against the national trend, and Labour lost Slough council after 15 years of majority control: the Tories finished with 21 seats against 18 Labour and 3 Lib Dems, and they formed a minority administraiton with Lib Dem support. Slough then came off the thirds electoral cycle, so there have been no local elections here since. One of the Labour casualties in 2023 was the council leader James Swindlehurst, and his presence on the ballot may well have been decisive in the Conservative gain of Cippenham Green ward. Cippenham Green, which lies at the western end of the town between the A4 Bath Road and the M4 motorway, is Slough's only remaining village green; it is also the only remaining open space in Cippenham Green ward which is otherwise entirely built-up. The ward makes the top 100 in England and Wales for Sikhs, who make up 9.1% of the population according to the 2021 census. Cippenham Green ward was significantly reduced in size at the 2023 election, with a corresponding reduction from three councillors to two. Its only previous poll was in 2023, when the Conservatives gained what had previously been a safe Labour ward: the Tories polled 44% against 28% for the Labour slate and 13% for an independent candidate. There was a similar Labour vote collapse in the 2024 general election in the Slough constituency, but the seat's Labour MP Tan Dhesi held on against split opposition to win his third term of office. Following the 2024 election Dhesi became chair of the Defence select committee. As stated the 2023 Slough elections returned 21 Conservatives, 18 Labour councillors and 3 Lib Dems. This is the first by-election since then, but it appears that there have been quite a lot of defections. In particular, seven Labour councillors quit the party over Gaza in the run-up to the 2024 general election; initially they formed an independent breakaway group, but they then joined the Liberal Democrats in 2025. This and other changes have left the latest composition as 19 Conservatives plus this vacancy facing 11 Labour councillors, 10 Lib Dems and an independent. The Conservative-Lib Dem agreement has apparently broken down, so this by-election could be a rather crucial one. It's to replace Conservative councillor Robert Stedmond, an Irishman who was first elected in 2023 and was the council's cabinet member for housing. Stedmond tendered his resignation in April for undisclosed reasons. Deefnding this seat for the Conservatives is Raja Muhammad Fayyaz, who stood in Upton Lea ward in 2023; he is a businessman (he is listed as a director of a halal meat company) who is acting chairman of the party's Slough branch. Labour's Preston Brooker is a retired transport manager and a long-serving former councillor who represented Haymill ward from 2012 to 2014 and then Langley Kedermister ward from 2014 until he lost his seat in 2023; Brooker was mayor of Slough in the COVID-hit year of 2020-21, and he is currently chair of Britwell parish council. There is no independent candidate this time, but we do have other candidacies from Jen Simpson for the Lib Dems, Chloe Hart for the Green Party and Christina Mahboob for Reform UK.
