Apsley and Corner Hall

Dacorum council, Hertfordshire; caused by the resignation of councillor Toni Cox. We now travel southwards up the railway line from Chester to London, alighting at Hemel Hempstead. Here the West Coast Main Line, the Grand Union Canal and the A41 road all share the valley of the River Bulbourne, but just to the east of the station the Bulbourne flows into the River Gade. Just below this confluence we find Apsley, which was once a major papermaking centre. The Frogmore Paper Mill, located on an island here in the middle of the Gade, claims to be the world's oldest mechanised paper mill although it is now primarily run as a museum. All the other paper mills have gone and the canal is now primarily used for recreational purposes: to reflect this, a canal marina was opened at Apsley in 2003 opposite a former paper mill site. A large office complex for Hertfordshire county council straddles the southern boundary of this ward. Apsley railway station, on the West Coast Main Line, is served by two trains to Euston and Milton Keynes each hour; Hemel Hempstead station has a more frequent service. The railway station at Hemel Hempstead was always some distance from the town centre even before the development of the New Town, whose central shopping area is in the Gade valley to the north of here, just beyond the Magic Roundabout. To the south and south-east of the town centre we find the New Town housing of Corner Hall. This runs up to Jarman Park at the eastern end of the ward, where we find a shopping and leixure complex that includes an athletics track and a large indoor ski slope, the Snow Centre. Apsley and Corner Hall ward is split between two divisions of Hertfordshire county council. Aspley is included in the Hemel Hempstead Town division which voted Lib Dem in 2025, while Corner Hall is part of Hemel Hempstead South East which re-elected its independent county councillor last year. The Hemel Hempstead parliamentary seat was a Labour gain in the 2024 general election, and David Taylor has been the town's MP since then. Hemel Hempstead is the major town in the Dacorum local government district, which covers the western end of Hertfordshire and is named after a former ancient hundred of the county. The district had a Conservative majority for most of the 21st century, but this fell in 2023 when the Liberal Democrats took overall control with 28 councillors against 18 Conservatives, 3 Labour and 2 independents. Apsley and Corner Hall ward has voted for the winning party in all Dacorum elections since the current ward boundaries were introduced in 2007, and so it was a Lib Dem gain from the Conservatives in 2023; vote shares here that year were 49% for the Liberal Democrats, 33% for the Conseratives and 19% for Labour. However, the Lib Dem majority here didn't last very long. The party group split in 2024, with eight female Lib Dem councillors walking out amid allegations of bullying, harassment and sexual harassment, and the remainder of the party group has been running Dacorum council as a minority since then. Other changes - including a Lib Dem by-election loss to the Conservatives in the neighbouring Hemel Hempstead Town ward in 2024, and a rare Labour by-election gain - mean that the latest composition has 17 Lib Dem councillors opposed by 18 Conservatives, 11 independents, 4 Labour and this seat which was vacated by Toni Cox in April. Cox had been part of the breakaway group in 2024. The Lib Dems will presumably want their defection loss back and they have selected Hugo Hardy, who is an architect. The Conseratives have selected Hubert Wilson. Labour's Bailey Hewitt is currently combining her studies for a master's degree in democracy and comparative politics at UCL with doing Parliamentary casework. Also on the ballot are Andrew Garnett for Reform UK; independent candidate Eric Johnson is a former TV presenter (The Wright Stuff, Bloomberg, Sky News) and producer who now runs his own production company; and the Green Party candidate is Connor Manship. Unusually, all of the six people standing in this by-election appear to be first-time candidates. Cippenham Green

Slough council, Berkshire; caused by the resignation of Conservative councillor Robert Stedmond. It's been a long time since Slough featured in this column - not because of the friendly bombs, but because there haven't been any by-elections here for years. The last by-election to Slough council was all the way back in May 2017; you have to go back to March 2012 for a poll that didn't coincide with ordinary local elections elsewhere. We have a lot of catching up to do. The story of Slough council over the last nine years has not been a happy one. To cut a long story short, the council declared bankruptcy in July 2021 and the Commissioners were sent in. They remain in place. Central government then pushed the factory reset button on Slough council, ordering a whole-council election in 2023 on a new electoral map. The 2023 Slough election then delivered huge gains for the Conservatives against the national trend, and Labour lost Slough council after 15 years of majority control: the Tories finished with 21 seats against 18 Labour and 3 Lib Dems, and they formed a minority administraiton with Lib Dem support. Slough then came off the thirds electoral cycle, so there have been no local elections here since. One of the Labour casualties in 2023 was the council leader James Swindlehurst, and his presence on the ballot may well have been decisive in the Conservative gain of Cippenham Green ward. Cippenham Green, which lies at the western end of the town between the A4 Bath Road and the M4 motorway, is Slough's only remaining village green; it is also the only remaining open space in Cippenham Green ward which is otherwise entirely built-up. The ward makes the top 100 in England and Wales for Sikhs, who make up 9.1% of the population according to the 2021 census. Cippenham Green ward was significantly reduced in size at the 2023 election, with a corresponding reduction from three councillors to two. Its only previous poll was in 2023, when the Conservatives gained what had previously been a safe Labour ward: the Tories polled 44% against 28% for the Labour slate and 13% for an independent candidate. There was a similar Labour vote collapse in the 2024 general election in the Slough constituency, but the seat's Labour MP Tan Dhesi held on against split opposition to win his third term of office. Following the 2024 election Dhesi became chair of the Defence select committee. As stated the 2023 Slough elections returned 21 Conservatives, 18 Labour councillors and 3 Lib Dems. This is the first by-election since then, but it appears that there have been quite a lot of defections. In particular, seven Labour councillors quit the party over Gaza in the run-up to the 2024 general election; initially they formed an independent breakaway group, but they then joined the Liberal Democrats in 2025. This and other changes have left the latest composition as 19 Conservatives plus this vacancy facing 11 Labour councillors, 10 Lib Dems and an independent. The Conservative-Lib Dem agreement has apparently broken down, so this by-election could be a rather crucial one. It's to replace Conservative councillor Robert Stedmond, an Irishman who was first elected in 2023 and was the council's cabinet member for housing. Stedmond tendered his resignation in April for undisclosed reasons. Deefnding this seat for the Conservatives is Raja Muhammad Fayyaz, who stood in Upton Lea ward in 2023; he is a businessman (he is listed as a director of a halal meat company) who is acting chairman of the party's Slough branch. Labour's Preston Brooker is a retired transport manager and a long-serving former councillor who represented Haymill ward from 2012 to 2014 and then Langley Kedermister ward from 2014 until he lost his seat in 2023; Brooker was mayor of Slough in the COVID-hit year of 2020-21, and he is currently chair of Britwell parish council. There is no independent candidate this time, but we do have other candidacies from Jen Simpson for the Lib Dems, Chloe Hart for the Green Party and Christina Mahboob for Reform UK.
