Local Government and South Yorkshire Combined Authority Mayoral Elections - Thursday 2 May 2024

On Thursday 2 May 2024, elections will take place in all 21 of Barnsley Council’s wards (one councillor per ward), as well as an election for a South Yorkshire Combined Authority Mayor.

To vote in these elections you must be registered on the electoral register at the property you reside at.

All electors who are added to the electoral register by 1 March 2024 and are eligible to vote will be sent a polling card around 26 March 2024. Further polling cards will be sent out during April to those electors who have been added to the register after 1 March 2024. The deadline to register to vote in these elections is 12 midnight on Tuesday 16 April 2024.

You can register to vote on the GOV UK website.

Notice of election

  • Local government election
  • Combined Authority Mayoral Elections

Voter ID

You need to show photo ID when you vote at a polling station. You can find out which type of photo ID is accepted on the Electoral Commission website.

You can apply for a free voter ID document, known as a Voter Authority Certificate, if:

  • you don’t already have an accepted form of photo ID
  • you’re not sure whether your photo ID still looks like you

The deadline to apply for a Voter Authority Certificate in time for this election is 5pm on Wednesday 24 April 2024.

Find out more and apply for a Voter Authority Certificate.

Other ways to apply

You can also apply for a Voter Authority Certificate by post. Fill in the application form and send this to: Electoral Services, PO Box 634, Barnsley, S70 9GG. You can request a copy of the form by calling us on (01226) 773070.

You may also be able to apply in person by visiting Barnsley Town Hall, Church Street, Barnsley, S70 2TA.

If you need any help with applying for a Voter Authority Certificate, call us on (01226) 773070.

Other ways to vote

Voting by post

If you’re on the electoral register, you can apply to vote by post instead of voting at the polling station.

If you are not already a postal voter, we must receive your application to vote by post for this election by no later than 5pm on Wednesday 17 April 2024. 

Voting by proxy

You can also appoint someone you trust to go to the polling station and vote on your behalf. This is called a ‘proxy’ vote. Find out more about voting by proxy.

The deadline to apply for a proxy vote for this election is 5pm on Wednesday, 24 April 2024.  Please note that both you and your nominated proxy must be registered to vote in the UK before you can apply and your proxy will need to show their own acceptable form of photo ID when voting on your behalf.
