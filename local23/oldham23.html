Local Election 4 May 2023 - results

Council political makeup

All-out election

This year is Oldham’s first all-out election since May 2004.

This means all 60 seats are being contested.

Three councillors will be elected in every ward, with the results of each vote displayed in the tables below.

The councillor with the highest number of votes in each ward will serve four years as an elected member, while the councillor with the second highest number of votes will serve three years, and the councillor with the third highest number of votes will serve one year (there are no elections in 2025 so no one serves a two year term).

To indicate which councillor will receive which term, the results will be recorded as follows:

  • Elected – Candidate with highest number of votes 
  • Elected – Candidate with second highest number of votes
  • Elected – Candidate with third highest number of votes
