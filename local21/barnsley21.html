Local government elections 2021

Results of the local government elections held on 6 May 2021.

  • Total votes cast 52,527
  • Turnout 28.47%
  • Total postal: 32,265
  • 61.4% of votes were postal votes

The Labour Party has had its majority on Barnsley Council reduced to 35 seats. Labour lost one seat overall in the 21 wards contested. 

The political make-up of the 63-seat council is now:

  • Barnsley Independent Group: 1
  • The Conservative Party candidate: 3
  • Independent: 3
  • Labour Party: 49
  • Liberal Democrats: 7

Rockingham Ward

Number of registered electors 8,716. Total number of ballot papers received 2,781.  Turnout 31.98%

Spoilt ballots - 17

Counting resulted in a dead heat between Labour Party candidate Nicola Sumner and the Conservative Party candidate Roy Garratt.

Ballot papers were re-counted numerous times, but a deadlock was still not broken, so the result was decided by chance. This is standard practice when this happens. A coin was tossed – heads for Conservative and tails for Labour. The coin landed on tails.
